Pluto: for happier, healthier pet families

Pluto is a Ruby on Rails Application built as a graduation Project at Le Wagon Tokyo. The app was meant to be a way to safely store information about your pet and to easily share it with family and pet sitters alike. To view the full product pitch I created for Pluto, please click here.

Our Goals for Pluto

As a team, we set a few important goals when developing our app. One was to use a brand new technology, not introduced by the bootcamp and something none of us have ever touched. For this we made use of the LINE Messanger Api. This Api gave us the ability to send direct messages to users LINE accounts so that they could always have the latest alerts about their pet without even opening pluto. Another major goal, was the ability to add multiple users to one specific pet. With this feature added, users are easily able to add a pet sitter to the account and make sure they had all the information available to safely watch over the pet(s).

Project Stack

During the creation of our project, we used a collection of different technologies to best build what we needed. In the earliest stages of our porject figma and figjam were used to map out our plans for the user interface and relational models. As the front end lead, I also took care of UX/UI and conducted user research, and worked on the main design's for pluto. The designs were then put into action with rails, html and vanilla javascript.

Our back end was then made primarily with ruby on rails, database management with MySql and hosting done on Heroku.

Problems faced

One of the major problems our team faced was building the schema for pluto. We wanted easch user to have a "pet-family", consisting of individual pets, and each pet would have a sub selection [meals, journals, medicine, appointments]. But what made things more difficult was that we also wanted to make it so multiple users could "share" one pet.

Our initial assumption (which cost us 5 days of work) was that a pet, belonged to a user. And that a pets "sub-selections" belonged to the pets. But then how do we allow multiple users to share one pet? After days of trouble shooting we came to the conclusion that there needed to be an "ownership" between the users and the pets. One user may have many relationships and which may contain many pets. One relationship can have N:N, both many users and many pets. To see our final schema please check here.
